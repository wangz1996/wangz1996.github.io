About Me

I am an experimental particle physicist with a Ph.D. from the Tsung-Dao Lee Institute at Shanghai Jiao Tong University. Currently, I am a postdoctoral researcher at the School of Physics, University of Science and Technology of China. My research focuses primarily on electroweak physics analysis and the exploration of Beyond Standard Model (BSM) physics within the ATLAS experiment. I have also contributed to detector design and software development for projects such as DarkSHINE, CEPC, and VLAST Pathfinder.

During my postdoctoral work, I have focused on detector simulation and signal processing for the VLAST Pathfinder project, leading the development of a full-stack detector simulation software framework based on Geant4, and applying deep learning algorithms (such as DGCNN) for electron-photon discrimination. Additionally, in the AHCAL energy reconstruction deep learning system project, I designed neural networks based on three-dimensional detector structures, achieving a 20%-30% improvement in detector resolution across the full energy range.

In my future research, I aim to contribute to solving computational and data challenges for future experiments (HL-LHC, FCC, etc.), with a focus on software-related technologies, such as data analysis systems and novel applications of machine learning in experimental high-energy physics.
